Attieke

Description

Attiéké (often spelled attieke) is a staple from Côte d’Ivoire made from fermented cassava. The grains are light, slightly tangy, and fluffy—similar in spirit to couscous, but with a character all their own. It is often served with grilled or fried fish and a fresh tomato and onion salsa called kedjenou style sauce or a simple onion–tomato topping.

This page describes a practical home version using dried attieke, which is widely sold in African markets and online. Once rehydrated and steamed, it makes an impressive centerpiece for a weeknight dinner or a gathering with friends.

Ingredients

  • 500 g (about 1 lb) dried attieke
  • Warm water, for soaking
  • 2–3 tbsp neutral oil (or red palm oil, if you prefer)
  • Salt, to taste
  • 4 firm fish fillets or whole small fish (tilapia, sea bream, or similar)
  • 4 large tomatoes, diced
  • 2 medium onions, thinly sliced
  • 2–3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1–2 hot chilies, sliced (optional)
  • 1 fish or vegetable stock cube (optional), crumbled
  • Fresh lime or lemon wedges, for serving

Steps

  1. Place the dried attieke in a large bowl. Cover with warm water and let soak until the grains soften, about 15–20 minutes, then drain very well in a sieve.
  2. Toss the drained attieke with a pinch of salt and 1 tablespoon of oil so the grains separate easily.
  3. Set up a steamer or a colander over simmering water. Steam the attieke until heated through and fluffy, about 15–25 minutes, fluffing with a fork once or twice.
  4. Meanwhile, pat the fish dry, season with salt (and a little pepper if you like), and fry or grill until cooked through and golden.
  5. In a wide pan, heat the remaining oil over medium heat. Cook the onions until soft, add garlic and chili, then stir in the tomatoes and stock cube (if using). Simmer until the sauce thickens slightly.
  6. Season the sauce to taste. Serve the steamed attieke with the fish and tomato–onion mixture on top, with lime or lemon on the side.
